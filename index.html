Why Geography Matters

Geography isn't just about maps; it's about understanding how our world works. From the tectonic movements that create mountains like Fuji to the climate cycles that shape the Sahara, everything is connected.

This archive serves as a Class 8 resource to help students visualize the physical features of the Earth through high-definition imagery and scientific profiles.

Key Earth Systems

Fundamental concepts for students

Lithosphere

The rigid outer part of the earth, consisting of the crust and upper mantle. Home to mountains, volcanoes, and plateaus.

Hydrosphere

All the waters on the earth's surface, such as lakes and seas, and sometimes including water over the earth's surface, such as clouds.

Biosphere

The regions of the surface, atmosphere, and hydrosphere of the earth occupied by living organisms. Biodiversity is key to survival.
